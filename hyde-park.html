About Hyde Park

Hyde Park is one of London's eight Royal Parks and covers 350 acres of green space in the heart of the city. Established by Henry VIII in 1536 for hunting, it opened to the public in 1637 and has since become one of London's most popular attractions.

The park is divided by the Serpentine and the Long Water lakes and features famous landmarks including Speaker's Corner, the Diana Memorial Fountain, and numerous statues and memorials. It hosts major events throughout the year including concerts, festivals, and winter celebrations.

Hyde Park has witnessed many important historical events including the Great Exhibition of 1851. The park's layout was largely designed by Decimus Burton in the 1820s. It's home to over 4,000 trees, including London plane trees, oaks, and horse chestnuts that create a beautiful canopy throughout the seasons.
