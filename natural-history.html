About the Museum

The Natural History Museum in London is a world-class visitor attraction and leading science research centre. Housing over 80 million specimens spanning billions of years, the museum welcomes more than 5 million visitors annually to explore everything from dinosaurs to diamonds.

The museum's iconic Romanesque architecture, designed by Alfred Waterhouse, is as impressive as the collections within. The cathedral-like Hintze Hall, with its dramatic blue whale skeleton suspended from the ceiling, sets the stage for an unforgettable journey through natural history.

Beyond public exhibitions, the museum is a world-leading research institution with over 300 scientists working on topics from biodiversity to mineralogy. The collections include specimens collected by Charles Darwin, priceless gemstones, and the first T-Rex fossil ever discovered.
