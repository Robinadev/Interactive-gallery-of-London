About St. Paul's Cathedral

St. Paul's Cathedral is an Anglican cathedral with a dome that has dominated the London skyline for over 300 years. The present building, dating from the late 17th century, was designed in the English Baroque style by Sir Christopher Wren.

The cathedral sits on the highest point in the City of London and is one of the most famous and recognizable sights of the capital. Its construction was part of a major rebuilding program in the City after the Great Fire of London in 1666.

üé® Architectural Brilliance

St. Paul's is noted for its dome, which rises 365 feet (111 meters) to the cross at its summit, making it one of the highest cathedral domes in the world. The dome is actually three separate structures: an outer timber-framed dome covered with lead, a decorative inner brick cone, and between them a structural brick cone which supports the stone lantern.
