Tower Bridge

Iconic Victorian Bridge spanning the River Thames

Historical Background

Tower Bridge was completed in 1894 after 8 years of construction, designed by Horace Jones and John Wolfe Barry. The bridge was built to ease road traffic while maintaining river access to the Pool of London docks. Its Victorian Gothic style was chosen to harmonize with the nearby Tower of London.

The bridge has become one of London's most recognizable symbols and has witnessed countless historical events, royal processions, and daily London life for over a century.

Architectural Features

Tower Bridge is a combined bascule and suspension bridge. The bascules (moveable roadways) weigh over 1,000 tons each and can be raised to an 86-degree angle in about 5 minutes. The bridge's towers are constructed of steel and clad in Cornish granite and Portland stone, standing 65 meters above the Thames.

The high-level walkways between the towers offer spectacular views of London and house the Tower Bridge Exhibition, showcasing the bridge's history and engineering.

Visitor Information

The Tower Bridge Exhibition offers visitors the chance to explore the bridge's history and walk across the high-level glass walkways for spectacular views of London. The Victorian Engine Rooms showcase the original steam engines that once powered the bridge lifts.

Visitors can also witness the bridge lifting for passing ships - check the official website for lift times. The best viewing spots are from the nearby riverbanks or from one of the many Thames river cruises.
