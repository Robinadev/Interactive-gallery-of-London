London Eye

Europe's Tallest Cantilevered Observation Wheel

Millennium Marvel

The London Eye was officially opened in 2000 as the Millennium Wheel, designed by architects David Marks and Julia Barfield. It was originally intended as a temporary attraction but proved so popular it became a permanent fixture on London's skyline.

Construction was a major engineering feat - the wheel was assembled horizontally over the Thames and raised into position, a process that took a week. It quickly became one of the UK's most popular paid tourist attractions.

Engineering Excellence

Standing 135 meters (443 feet) tall, the London Eye is the world's tallest cantilevered observation wheel. It offers 360-degree views of London extending up to 40 kilometers on clear days.

The wheel rotates at 0.9 km/h - slow enough for passengers to walk on and off while it's moving. Each of the 32 sealed capsules represents one of London's boroughs and can hold 25 people. The wheel is supported by tensioned steel cables and rests on two 300-tonne foundations.

Visitor Experience

A standard rotation takes 30 minutes, giving you ample time to enjoy the breathtaking views. On clear days, you can see Windsor Castle, 40 kilometers away. Each capsule is equipped with interactive guides pointing out landmarks.

For special occasions, consider a private capsule or champagne experience. The London Eye is particularly spectacular at sunset and when illuminated at night. Fast-track tickets are available to skip the queues during peak times.
