British Museum

2 Million Years of Human History & Culture

Museum History

The British Museum was established in 1753, largely based on the collections of physician and scientist Sir Hans Sloane. It first opened to the public in 1759 in Montagu House, making it the first national public museum in the world.

The current building was designed in the Greek Revival style by Robert Smirke and completed in 1852. The museum's collection has grown to over 8 million works, spanning 2 million years of human history across all continents.

Architectural Marvel

The museum's most famous architectural feature is the Great Court, with its spectacular glass and steel roof designed by Norman Foster. Completed in 2000, it is the largest covered public square in Europe at 2 acres.

The Reading Room at the center dates from 1857 and has hosted famous readers including Karl Marx, Virginia Woolf, and Mahatma Gandhi. The Greek Revival facade features 44 Ionic columns, each 45 feet high.

Must-See Exhibits

Don't miss the Rosetta Stone (key to deciphering Egyptian hieroglyphs), the Parthenon Sculptures from Athens, the Egyptian mummies, the Lewis Chessmen, and the Sutton Hoo treasure. The museum offers free guided tours and excellent audio guides.

With over 60 galleries, it's impossible to see everything in one visit. Focus on your interests: Egyptian, Greek, Roman, Middle Eastern, or the extensive collection of prints and drawings.
